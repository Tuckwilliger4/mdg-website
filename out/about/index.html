ABOUT US

McKim Design Group was founded in south San Jose in 1996 by Kirk S. McKim. Since then we have followed a “general-practice” business model providing design and management services in all sectors of the industry including residential, commercial, tenant improvement, restaurant, civic, K-12 educational, and post-secondary educational sectors.

Our project experience range from as small as school restroom remodels, as complex and environmentally conscious as the LEED GOLD certified City Hall for Rohnert Park, and as large as full campus modernizations and new schools. McKim Design Group endeavors to provide a professional yet enjoyable atmosphere that extends from its staff and office to every project they undertake. We find that because of the time and energy the entire team and owners invest, the process must not only be successful but fun.

Leadership

Kirk McKim

Principal-in-Charge

A proven leader with a dynamic career in Architecture spanning over 30 years, Mr. McKim has extensive experience in California with a very diverse clientele and project portfolio. His background working for a large firm specializing in health-care and amusement park projects as well as his private sector experience at a small firm allowed Mr. McKim to assist in opening a local office for a major national firm. Eventually, Mr. McKim missed the hands-on day-to-day interaction with clients and time spent in the practice of architecture that only a small firm can provide. Thus, in 1996 he opened McKim Design Group, a general practice firm focused on complete project management from start to finish with principal involvement throughout all phases of every project.

Todd Walter

Project Director

Mr. Walter has 30 years of experience in all aspects of educational design including programming, feasibility studies, master planning, design, cost-estimating and construction administration. He has also led many community programming sessions and presentations over his career. Mr. Walter has experience working with many of the state agencies that govern K-12 facilities including Division of the State Architect (DSA), Office of Public School Construction (OPSC) and others including CDE, DTSC and the Health Department.

Emeline Oliveri

Designer & Project Manager

An outstanding communicator with enduring client relationships and excellent interpersonal skills, Mrs. Oliveri is a proven leader of architectural programming efforts and conceptualization within the K-12 design team. Her background in Art History allows a unique perspective on design and lends to her skills in interior design and space planning. Mrs. Oliveri’s tenure — extending back to 2004 — with McKim Design Group has honed her knowledge of building construction and detailing across a wide range of educational and residential projects and reinforced her position as a cardinal team member for K-12 construction administration.
